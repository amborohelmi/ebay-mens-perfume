⚑ Strategic Priorities — 3 Immediate Actions

01

Double Down on Value-EDT

Concentrate inventory in the $25–$50 Eau de Toilette segment. It holds 240 listings generating $13.8M. The avg sold of 1,460 units at $36 avg price = a predictable, high-velocity formula. Target brands: Calvin Klein, Davidoff, Azzaro, Burberry.

02

Tester Strategy Arbitrage

Listings with "tester" in title average 2,159 units sold — 4.6× the market average of 766. Testers are priced ~15–20% below retail but convert massively. For fragrance-agnostic buyers, tester listings = highest ROI channel with minimal additional cost.

03

Geographic Diversification

New Jersey holds 63% of revenue from just ~18% of listings — a wholesale hub signal. If competing, you must match NJ sellers' scale and pricing. Alternatively, underserved states (California, Florida) have high listing counts but disproportionately low revenue — premium opportunity in neglected markets.

Listing Keyword Intelligence
Title keywords that drive highest average units sold

⚑ Brand Portfolio Recommendations for the CEO

A

Invest: Volume-Value Play

Build depth in Davidoff, Calvin Klein, Versace, Azzaro. These brands have proven they can sell 2,500–7,800 units at $23–$43 price points. Focus on 3.4oz and 6.7oz tester + new-in-box listings. Target: $5M revenue from these 4 brands alone with 40–50 total listings.

B

Grow: Premium-Volume Hybrids

Expand Montblanc, HUGO BOSS, Carolina Herrera, Gucci — all achieve above-median volume at above-median price. These are margin enhancers. Add 5–10 targeted SKUs per brand, focusing on their signature hero products. Expected revenue uplift: +$1.2M per quarter.

C

Defend: Listing Optimization

Audit current Giorgio Armani, Paco Rabanne, Ralph Lauren listings — high listing count, low velocity. Consider: (1) repricing to $30–$40 range for volume recovery, (2) converting to tester listings (+4.6× avg sold), (3) consolidating SKUs to the 3.4oz "New in Box" format — the single highest-volume listing template.
